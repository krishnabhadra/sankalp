Who's This For

THE ELITE
RESEARCH
EXPERIENCE.

Designed exclusively for early-stage graduate students serious about contributing to the global robotics research community.

This bootcamp is for M.Tech, M.S., and Ph.D. Year 1–2 students who have strong academic foundations but need structured guidance to transition into active research — from identifying problems to formulating proposals to publishing in IEEE venues.

Every participant leaves with a formal 2-page research proposal evaluated by a faculty panel, and top proposals are selected for poster presentation at the NeuroFrontiers 2027 Conclave.

"The Graduate Research Bootcamp is the defining element of SANKALP's Main Track — it bridges coursework and active research, preparing participants to contribute to the global robotics research community."

5-Day Journey

5 DAYS.
ONE MISSION.

A structured progression from research methodology through frontier topics to a formal, faculty-evaluated research proposal.

01
Research Methodology in Robotics Day 1
  • Research problem identification, literature review strategies, and gap analysis
  • IEEE paper anatomy — writing abstracts, introductions, and related work sections
  • Experimental design, reproducibility, and statistical analysis for robotics
02
Advanced Neurorobotics Research Frontiers Day 2
  • EEG/EMG signal decoding — feature extraction (time, frequency, wavelet) and classification (SVM, CNN, LSTM)
  • BCI paradigms: P300, SSVEP, Motor Imagery — and the ethics of neurotechnology
  • Neuroprosthetics research: dexterous hands, sensory feedback, exoskeleton control
03
AI & Machine Learning for Intelligent Robotics Day 3
  • Deep learning architectures for EEG/EMG — transfer learning and biosignal data augmentation
  • Reinforcement learning for adaptive prosthetic control and sim-to-real transfer
  • Hands-on paper reading workshop — 3 recent IEEE RA-L / ICRA papers, GSAC-facilitated
04
Research Problem Formulation & Prototyping Day 4
  • Scoping tractable research questions — gap analysis, feasibility, and 6-month timelines
  • Research prototype design principles — instrumentation, logging, and version control
  • Team proposal drafting session — 2–3 students per team, mentored by GSAC and faculty
05
Presentations & NeuroFrontiers Preparation Day 5
  • 10-minute formal proposal presentations + 5-minute Q&A, evaluated by faculty panel
  • Top proposals selected for NeuroFrontiers 2027 poster — with poster design guidance
  • IEEE RAS career pathways: PhD opportunities, conferences, publications, and grants
The Final Output

PROPOSE.
PRESENT.
GET SELECTED.

The bootcamp culminates in formal research proposal presentations — evaluated by a multi-institutional faculty panel.

YOUR RESEARCH.
THEIR STAGE.

Teams of 2–3 graduate students present a 2-page formal research proposal covering motivation, related work, methodology, expected outcomes, and a realistic timeline.

Proposals are evaluated on novelty, feasibility, methodology, potential impact, and clarity. The best are selected to present as posters at the NeuroFrontiers 2027 Conclave — putting your research in front of the wider IEEE RAS community.
