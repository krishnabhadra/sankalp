INTRODUCTION
With the evolution of robotics from industrial automation to intelligent, adaptive, and
human-centered systems, the ability of machines to interpret and respond to human signals
biological, visual, and auditory has emerged as a critical area of innovation. This track focuses
on neurorobotics and intelligent interaction systems, integrating robotics, artificial
intelligence, and human–machine communication.
The program adopts a progressive learning framework, beginning with a structured online
foundational series, followed by expert-led sessions, and culminating in an intensive hands-on
workshop series. This approach ensures that participants transition from conceptual
understanding to practical implementation and system-level thinking.
LEARNING OUTCOMES
By the end of the program, participants will:
● Develop a strong foundation in robotics, AI, and human–robot interaction
● Understand and differentiate between multi-modal signals (bio-signals, vision, and audio)
16
● Gain hands-on experience in designing human-interactive robotic systems
● Explore applications in assistive robotics and neuroprosthetics
● Build, test, and present functional prototypes

WORKSHOP OBJECTIVE
To enable participants to develop human-centered robotic systems through parallel,

WORKSHOP STRUCTURE
Participants are divided into three tracks at the beginning of the workshop. Each track
progresses simultaneously through system design, development, integration, and demonstration,
guided by dedicated mentors.
TRACK 1: Neurorobotics & Multimodal Bio-Signal Control
EMG-Driven Assistive Robotic System with Vision-Assisted
Interaction
INTRODUCTION
19
This track focuses on neurorobotics and the decoding of human physiological signals into robotic
actions. Participants will learn core principles of bio-signal acquisition, neural intent
interpretation, and assistive system design, with a special emphasis on electromyography
(EMG)-based control systems.
To enhance system intelligence and usability, vision-based gesture recognition is integrated as a
secondary input, enabling context-aware and intuitive human–robot interaction.
The workshop simulates real-world assistive technologies such as prosthetic limbs, rehabilitation
devices, and human-augmentation systems where robots respond directly to human intent.
DAY 1: Bio-Signal Acquisition & Neural Interface Setup
Objective: Establish a foundational neurorobotic interface using EMG signals.
Technical Activities:
● Introduction to neurorobotics and bio-signal-based control
● Understanding EMG signal characteristics: amplitude, noise, variability
● Sensor interfacing and real-time analog signal acquisition with Arduino
● EMG signal calibration (muscle contraction vs baseline noise)
● Signal visualization via Arduino Serial Plotter
Learning Outcome:
Participants build a functional neural interface layer, enabling robotic actuation based on human
muscle signals.
DAY 2: Neural Control Systems & Multimodal Integration
Objective: Develop a robust neurorobotic control system with enhanced responsiveness.
Core Neurorobotics:
● Signal filtering to reduce EMG noise and artifacts
● Threshold tuning and adaptive control strategies
● Multi-state control logic for graded actuation (e.g., grip strength levels)
20
● Precision servo motor calibration
Integration Activities:
● Serial communication between Python (vision system) and Arduino
● Gesture-based supervisory control (mode switching, safety override)
● Fusion of EMG (primary control) and vision (secondary/contextual control)
Learning Outcome:
A refined neurorobotic control system capable of interpreting human intent with improved
accuracy and stability.
DAY 3: Assistive System Deployment & Validation
Objective: Implement and evaluate a complete neurorobotic assistive system.
Technical Activities:
● End-to-end system testing (EMG input → signal processing → actuation)
● Performance validation: accuracy, repeatability, response time
● Debugging and system optimization
Learning Outcome:
A fully functional neurorobotic prototype demonstrating:
● Bio-signal-driven control
● Human intent decoding
● Assistive robotic application
LEARNING IMPACT
Participants will gain:
● A strong foundation in neurorobotics and bio-signal processing
● Hands-on experience with EMG-based control systems
● Insight into human–machine neural interfaces
● Exposure to assistive robotics and rehabilitation technologies
● The ability to design real-world neurorobotic solutions
TRACK 2: Haptics & Tactile Human–Robot Interaction
Project: Haptic-Feedback Teleoperation System
DAY 1: Tactile Sensing & Data Acquisition
Objective: Capture physical interaction data from the robot
21
Technical Activities:
● Interfacing Force Sensitive Resistors (FSRs) with microcontrollers
● Calibrating pressure sensors for grip and contact detection
● Signal conditioning and noise reduction for tactile data
● Visualizing real-time pressure maps on a digital interface
DAY 2: Haptic Rendering & Actuator Control
Objective: Translate robotic contact into human sensation
Technical Activities:
● Driving Linear Resonant Actuators (LRA) or vibration motors
● Mapping force intensity to vibration frequency/amplitude
● Implementing haptic drivers (e.g., DRV2605L) for distinct tactile effects
● Developing "haptic icons" (e.g., double-pulse for collision, steady buzz for contact)
● Minimizing the latency between sensor input and tactile output
DAY 3: Closed-Loop Validation & Demonstration
Objective: Achieve intuitive "blind" control via touch feedback
Technical Activities:
● End-to-end testing (Robot contact → Processing → Wearable feedback)
● Testing sensory substitution (using touch to guide movement without looking)
TRACK 3: Adaptive Cyber-Physical Systems for Industrial Safety
& Smart Environments
Project Theme: Intelligent Safety & Monitoring CPS Node
PROGRAM OVERVIEW
This track focuses on the design and implementation of cyber-physical systems (CPS) that
integrate real-world sensing, edge-level intelligence, and cloud connectivity to enable
autonomous monitoring and safety-oriented decision-making.
Participants will develop a smart CPS node capable of:
● Detecting environmental conditions
● Performing real-time local control
● Communicating with cloud systems
22
● Responding to safety-critical scenarios
The track emphasizes closed-loop system design, resilience, and real-world industrial relevance.
DAY 1: Physical Sensing & Edge-Level Intelligence
Objective: To establish a foundational cyber-physical system through real-time sensing and local
decision-making.
Technical Focus Areas
CPS Fundamentals
● Introduction to cyber-physical systems architecture
● Understanding the sensing → processing → actuation loop
Sensor Integration & Data Acquisition
● Interfacing environmental sensors (e.g., temperature, light, gas, or proximity)
● Real-time data acquisition and calibration
● Basic signal conditioning and noise handling
Edge-Level Control Logic
● Threshold-based and rule-based decision-making
● Local automation strategies for safety and environmental response
● Implementation of manual override mechanisms
Learning Outcome
Participants build a self-regulating CPS node capable of sensing environmental conditions and
executing local control actions.
DAY 2: Cyber Integration & Digital Monitoring Systems
Objective: To extend the physical system into a connected CPS through cloud integration and
real-time monitoring.
Technical Focus Areas
Connectivity & Communication
● Establishing wireless communication between edge device and cloud platform
● Introduction to lightweight communication protocols (e.g., MQTT or similar)
Cloud Dashboard & Visualization
● Development of a real-time monitoring interface
23
● Visualization of environmental data streams
Bi-Directional Interaction
● Remote monitoring of system state
● Remote triggering of control actions
System Enhancement
● Data smoothing and filtering for stable system behavior
● Event-driven alerts for abnormal conditions (e.g., threshold violations)
Learning Outcome
Participants develop a connected CPS system with real-time monitoring and remote interaction
capabilities, forming a basic digital representation of the physical system.
DAY 3: Adaptive Control, System Resilience & Deployment
Objective: To implement an intelligent, resilient CPS capable of operating reliably under
real-world conditions.
Technical Focus Areas
Adaptive System Behavior
● Dynamic control strategies based on environmental trends
● Context-aware decision-making
System Reliability & Fault Tolerance
● Designing fail-safe mechanisms
● Ensuring system functionality under communication loss or sensor anomalies
Full System Integration
● End-to-end pipeline:
Sensing → Edge Processing → Cloud Interaction → Actuation
