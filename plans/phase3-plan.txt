PHASE III – GRADUATE RESEARCH BOOTCAMP
The Graduate Research Bootcamp is the defining element of SANKALP's Main Track
alignment. It is a 5-day intensive research-oriented program held at IIT Palakkad, designed
exclusively for early-stage graduate students (M.Tech / M.S. / Ph.D. Year 1-2). It bridges the gap
between coursework and active research, preparing participants to contribute to the global
robotics research community.

Day 1: Research Methodology in Robotics
● Session 1: The Research Process in Robotics & Automation
○ Identification of research problems in neurorobotics, haptics, and CPS.
○ Literature review strategies: IEEE Xplore, Google Scholar, and citation mapping.
○ Distinguishing between State-of-the-Art (SOTA) and research gaps.
○ Formulating precise research questions and hypotheses.
● Session 2: Writing for IEEE Publications
○ Anatomy of an IEEE conference and journal paper.
○ Writing abstracts, introductions, and related work sections.
○ Identifying and avoiding common pitfalls in technical writing.
○ Hands-on: Participants draft a 150-word abstract on an assigned topic.
● Session 3: Experimental Design & Validation
○ Designing reproducible robotics experiments.
○ Key metrics: Accuracy, latency, reliability, and usability in neurorobotic systems.
○ Dataset creation, annotation, and ethical data handling protocols.
○ Statistical analysis techniques for robotics experimentation.
Day 2: Advanced Neurorobotics Research Frontiers
● Session 1: EEG/EMG Decoding — State of the Art
○ Signal acquisition pipelines for research-grade EEG/EMG systems.
○ Feature extraction methods: Time-domain, frequency-domain, and wavelet
methods.
○ Classification approaches: LDA, SVM, CNN, and LSTM for biosignal decoding.
○ Open research challenges: Subject independence, noise robustness, and real-time
constraints.
● Session 2: Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) — Research Perspectives
○ BCI paradigms: P300, SSVEP, and Motor Imagery.
27
○ Closed-loop BCI systems: Bridging the gap from signal to action.
○ Neural plasticity and the development of adaptive BCI.
○ The ethical landscape and considerations of neurotechnology research.
● Session 3: Neuroprosthetics & Assistive Robotics Research
○ Current research trends in dexterous prosthetic hands.
○ Restoring sensory feedback in prosthetic systems.
○ Exoskeleton control logic and rehabilitation robotics.
○ Case Studies: Reviewing published research from IIT Madras, IIIT Hyderabad,
and international labs.
Day 3: AI & Machine Learning for Intelligent Robotic Systems
● Session 1: Deep Learning for Biosignal Classification
○ CNN architectures specifically designed for EEG/EMG data.
○ Transfer learning techniques for subject-independent modeling.
○ Data augmentation strategies tailored for biosignal datasets.
● Session 2: Reinforcement Learning (RL) for Adaptive Robotics
○ RL Fundamentals: Rewards, policies, and environment interaction.
○ Applications in adaptive prosthetic control and rehabilitation robotics.
○ Addressing Sim-to-Real transfer challenges.
● Session 3: Hands-on Paper Reading Workshop
○ Analysis of 3 recent IEEE RA-L / ICRA-affiliated papers on assigned topics.
○ Structured Reading: Problem identification, methodology, results, and
limitations.
○ Group discussion facilitated by GSAC members to critique and connect to
personal research interests.
Day 4: Research Problem Formulation & Prototyping
● Session 1: Identifying Your Research Problem
○ Transitioning from general interest to a specific, tractable research question.
○ Gap analysis: Identifying what can be achieved within a 6-month timeline.
○ Scoping: Assessing feasibility, available resources, and realistic timelines.
● Session 2: Prototyping for Research vs. Prototyping for Demo
○ Principles of research prototype design.
○ Instrumentation and data logging requirements for reproducibility.
○ Best practices for version control, documentation, and open-source contributions.
28
● Session 3: Team Research Proposal Work Session
○ Teams (2–3 graduate students) begin drafting a 2-page formal research proposal.
○ Mentorship provided by GSAC members and faculty mentors.
○ Structure: Motivation, related work, methodology, expected outcomes, and
timeline.
Day 5: Research Proposal Presentations & NeuroFrontiers Preparation
● Session 1: Research Proposal Presentations
○ Formal presentations: 10-minute presentation followed by 5-minute Q&A.
○ Evaluation by a faculty panel (IIT Palakkad, IIIT Kottayam, and NIT Calicut).
○ Criteria: Novelty, feasibility, methodology, potential impact, and clarity.
● Session 2: Poster Preparation for NeuroFrontiers Conclave
○ Selection of top proposals for poster presentation at NeuroFrontiers 2027.
○ Guidance on academic poster design and effective presentation skills.
● Session 3: Research Pathways & IEEE RAS Opportunities
○ Overview of PhD opportunities in robotics (India and International).
○ Guide to IEEE RAS conferences: ICRA, IROS, RO-MAN, and Humanoids.
○ Publication pathways: IEEE RA-L, TRO, and TASE.
○ Information on RAS funding, travel grants, and Technical Committee (TC)
involvement.
