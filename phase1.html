Setting the Context

WHAT IS
NEUROROBOTICS?

The science of connecting human signals — brain waves, muscle impulses, gestures — to robotic systems that sense, respond, and assist.

🧠
Brain Signal
→
⚡

🤖
Robot Action

From prosthetics that read muscle signals to robots that respond to hand gestures — neurorobotics is where neuroscience, AI, and robotics converge to build systems that truly understand humans. In Pragyan, students experience this fusion firsthand.

Pragyan · Hands-on Robotics · Grades 8–12
Program Journey

3 DAYS.
ONE JOURNEY.

A carefully designed progression — from foundational concepts to hands-on robot building to real-world hospital exposure.

01
Foundations of Neurorobotics & Human–Machine Interaction Offline
  • Introduction to neurorobotics — how brain, muscle, voice, and gesture signals control machines
  • Hands-on breadboard circuit building with LEDs and switches
  • Live demos of gesture-controlled and clap-activated systems
02
Applied Robotics — Sensing, Control & Autonomous Systems Offline
  • Learn robotic sensing with ultrasonic/IR sensors and decision-making logic
  • Design and build an obstacle-avoiding robot from scratch
  • Test, optimize, and race robots through a navigation challenge
03
Experiential Learning — Neurorobotics in Real Life Field Visit
  • Visit a hospital or industry partner to see rehabilitation robotics in action
  • Observe the G-Gaitor robotic gait system by Genrobotics
  • Interact with doctors and engineers working at the frontier of healthcare robotics
Hands-On Highlights

BUILD IT.
TEST IT.
SEE IT LIVE.

This isn't a lecture series. Students construct circuits, assemble robots, and walk into real hospitals — learning by doing at every step.

🔌
Breadboard Circuit Lab
Day 1 · Electronics

Build a working circuit from scratch on a breadboard — batteries, LEDs, resistors, and switches. Understand how current flows and how simple control logic works.

🤖
Obstacle-Avoiding Robot
Day 2 · Robotics Build

Assemble a robot with motors, wheels, and ultrasonic sensors. Program it to detect and navigate around obstacles autonomously — then race it through a challenge course.

🏥
Hospital & Industry Visit
Day 3 · Field Exposure

Step into a real rehabilitation centre or industry lab. See robot-assisted gait systems in action and talk to the doctors and engineers who build them.

Real-World Exposure
